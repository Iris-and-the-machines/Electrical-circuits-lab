Chapter 4 Introduction

4.1 Aim of the lab

This set of problems is going to help you get familiar with Ohm’s law, which you will use extensively during the physiology course. On the one hand it helps you to understand how cells use electrical activity to communicate with each other. On the other hand it underlies the measurement techniques we are using to measure electrical properties of cells like action potentials, membrane potentials etc.

Our goal with this exercise is to help you think deeply about Ohm’s law and the properties of electrical circuits. Additionally we hope this exercise will also help you get more comfortable with electrical circuits and have some fun. You are very welcome to play around and try out things that are not mentioned in the lab manual.

4.2 What we’ll do

To get you started, we are using the most simple electrical circuit: An electrical signal (here potential difference) generated by a battery or by an adjustable power source (also called “function generator”).

Instead of subcellular elements, you will use metal wires. Another important difference is that, instead of ions moving to conduct electricity, we will deal with electrons moving to conduct electricity. However, the bottomline and conclusions are not affected by that.
